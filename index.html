Research

Sorp-vection transport theory

Extending the Damle–Koros framework by incorporating liquid-phase carrier gas solubility into the sorp-vection driving force. This reframes carrier gas selection (CO₂, N₂, Xe, SF₆) as a tunable design variable rather than a passive choice, opening new parametric space for module optimization.

PDMS hollow fiber platforms & module scale-up

Parametric studies of PDMS selective layer thickness, fiber count, and carrier gas selection for sorp-vection modules. Ongoing work on diagnosing and resolving performance changes during scale-up from lab to larger module configurations.

Surface modification of PDMS membranes

Plasma and chemical routes to introduce polar functionality onto PDMS surfaces, characterized by FTIR and XPS. Exploring masking strategies (PVA sacrificial lift-off) for selective coating of hollow fiber lumen surfaces.

Carbon molecular sieve (CMS) hollow fibers

Precursor chemistry and pyrolysis protocols for CMS hollow fibers derived from novel polyimide dianhydrides. Work includes fiber coating and blocking-risk mitigation prior to high-temperature treatment.
